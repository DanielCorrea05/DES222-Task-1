Verified City
by Daniel Correa
Surveillance isn’t just cameras on corners. It’s woven into purchases, movements, and even moods. You rarely notice it, until something feels off...
You decide with your partner to go camping this summer. That night, your phone shows ads for tents and portable barbecues while scrolling.
You start to wonder not just how things got this way, but how they might evolve...
If surveillance is this unnerving now, what could it look like in five, ten, twenty years? Could it become invisible? Could it become mandatory?
You then step into the future of a city that knows you. Billboards shift as you pass. A drone hovers briefly, scanning your gait. The sidewalk glows subtly, guiding your route.
Every surface is responsive. Your phone syncs with public infrastructure. You haven’t spoken to anyone, but the city has already made decisions on your behalf.
In this future, technology anticipates your needs, but also your moods, movements, and mistakes. You begin to wonder who’s really in control.
Synthetic Personas in Customer Service use emotionally manipulative scripts to guide you into accepting terms or avoiding complaints.
Predictive policing drones hover near individuals flagged by behavioral algorithms, even if no crime has occurred.
Dynamic pricing zones adjust costs of food, transport, or access based on your personal data profile and perceived “social value.”
